Who I work with

The settings vary. The problems people bring rarely do.

  • The organisations. Banks, airports, carmakers, cruise lines, universities, the police. Anywhere a lot of people have to pull in the same direction under pressure.
  • The situations. A culture that looks good on paper but doesn't match daily life. Teams that work hard without quite delivering. Leaders promoted for being good at the job, who now need to be good at leading.
  • The people. Usually whoever is responsible for an operation and the people running it: a chief executive, a director, a head of department, or the teams who support them.

What I do

Three kinds of work, usually blended to fit the organisation rather than sold off a shelf.

Consulting

Working out what kind of leadership and culture your organisation needs next, and what that means leaders should do differently on a Tuesday afternoon.

More about consulting

Facilitation

Sessions built around your people's real situations, not case studies. They practise the conversations they actually need to have, and leave with something to do next week.

More about facilitation

Coaching

One to one and team coaching that helps leaders see how they come across, what keeps happening under pressure, and what to do about it.

More about coaching

Why people work with me

And, more to the point, why they come back.

  • I go where the work happens. On ships, in kitchens, on factory floors, in real meetings. An hour of watching tells you more than a stack of survey results.
  • I look at the whole picture. The person, the team and the organisation around them. Problems rarely sit in just one.
  • I'm supportive and direct at the same time. People feel backed, and they hear things nobody else will tell them.
  • Talk turns into action. We agree what should look different afterwards, and how we'll both know whether it does.
