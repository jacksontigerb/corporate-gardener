How I work

The same approach, whatever the work.

Consulting, facilitation or coaching, this is what sits underneath all of it. Think of this page as the honest version of the brochure.

What I believe

Three convictions, earned over twenty five years of watching people lead.

  • Most leaders are more capable than their environment lets them be.
  • Culture is what leaders repeatedly tolerate, reward and role model.
  • People change what they do when they see themselves, and their situation, more clearly.

Three threads run through everything

  • The whole picture. I look at the person, the team and the organisation around them at the same time. A “difficult” manager often makes complete sense once you see the pressures above and around them.
  • Did anything actually change? What matters is what's different afterwards: the conversations people have, the decisions they make, the results. We agree at the start what better looks like and how we'll know.
  • Support and challenge. I'm on your side and straight with you. Both at once, because either one on its own doesn't work.

I watch, rather than just ask

Surveys and interviews tell you what people say. I put most of my weight on what people do, so I spend time where the work happens: real meetings, real shifts, real handovers.

Then I line up three versions of the story. What the organisation says about itself. What its leaders believe is going on. What I actually see. Where the three agree, you're probably fine. Where they don't is nearly always where the work is.

A note on recording

Sometimes I record sessions, using tools like Otter, so that feedback and reports quote what was actually said rather than what I happen to remember. That only ever happens with permission. You'll always know what's being recorded, why, and who will see it, and you can pause or stop at any point.

What it's like in the room

  • Direct, but respectful. Nobody gets ambushed, and nothing gets said about you that hasn't been said to you.
  • Curious, but going somewhere. Reflection is a means to a decision or an action, not an end in itself.
  • Flexible. If the most useful thing turns out not to be the thing on the plan, we do the useful thing.
  • Transparent. You always know which job I'm doing, coaching, advising or reporting, and exactly who gets to hear what.
