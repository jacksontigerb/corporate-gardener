# Corporate Gardener — product brief

## What this is

A one page marketing and portfolio site for **Corporate Gardener**, the executive
coaching and organisation development practice of **Nic Brocklebank**. It replaces
the old corporategardener.co.uk. The design is the product: a visitor's impression
in the first few seconds is the thing being made.

## Register

Brand. Design IS the product (landing page / portfolio), not app UI.

## Who it is for

Senior people who buy coaching and development: HR and people directors, heads of
talent and L&D, and senior leaders considering coaching for themselves or their
teams. They are discerning and risk aware. The site has to read as credible and
established first, distinctive second. Warmth and a clear point of view matter, but
never at the expense of trust.

## The one job

Get the right organisation to start a conversation. Everything points at the contact
form. Success is a considered enquiry landing in Nic's inbox, not raw traffic.

## Voice

Plain, warm, human, quietly confident. British spelling. No corporate filler, no
buzzwords, no inflated claims. The central metaphor carries the personality:
organisations are living gardens, and the work is to tend the conditions so people
grow, rather than to arrive as a mechanic with a toolkit of fixes.

## What must stay true

- The single brand green `#adb52e` from the logo. Monochrome plus that one green.
- The copy is real and specific (named clients, real credentials). Never invent
  experience or inflate a claim.
- Fast, accessible, and hostable anywhere: plain HTML, CSS and JS, no build step.

## Open content items (owner: Nic / Jackson)

- **Phone number** is still a placeholder (`[PHONE]` in the contact section). Add the
  real number or delete that line before launch.
- **Copy sign off:** client names and figures (e.g. the "£2bn" at Heathrow, "500+
  hours") come from Nic's public bio and should be read and confirmed by him.
- **Bespoke imagery** (optional upgrade): the hero currently uses a real greenhouse
  photograph, graded to sit in the palette. It can be swapped for commissioned or
  AI generated painterly garden art later without touching the layout.
